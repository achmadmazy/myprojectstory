Piping and Instrumentation Design (P&ID) using Visio Software

The surface production facilities are set up based on required design and capacity. The process includes production manifold, separation, recompression, oil pumping, waste water treatment and glycol dehydration. The proposed outcomes, the first fraction of treated gas will be used for lifting and the second fraction will be liquefied producing the NGL for sales. The oil that is separated will be the main source of profit for sales, and the treated water will be used for water injection to provide additional pressure support for reservoir:

1. Oil processing

The fluids that produced from reservoir passes the system of production manifold and separation process using horizontal separator. In separation stage, the fluids are promoted by the density difference and passes through four stages operating with different level of pressure. The first three stages are consisted of three phase to separate oil, water and gas and two phase separator a to separate either oil, water or gas and the last stage pass through the electrostatic coalescer to separate crude oil from water and contaminants through desalting and dehydration. The heat exchanger is introduced in most of process to achieve the facility’s efficiency. The export pumping system is used to export the oil from separator, where the oil is still mixed with condensate /vapour. The condensate is then removed by flash gas, cooled, pumped and stored to shipping using tanker through CALM 2 Gas Conditioning

2. Gas Conditioning

The gas recovered from separation process goes to recompression train to produce dry gas. The heat exchanger is introduced to cool the gas and scrubber is used to obtain liquid droplet from gas to recover oil and produce dry gas. The sweetening process is carried out for H2S removal using sulfinol absorption scheme with lean solvent of sulfolane as a mixture of tetrahydrothiophene dioxide and di-isopropanolamine as simpler regeneration, less expensive, lower circulation rates and elimination of stripper, however the flash separator is used to avoid expensive distillation colum and high running cost. The dehydration unit is introduced to recover the heavy hydrocarbon and water vapour achieved by a glycol absoption scheme. The lean solvent is used to mixed with wet gas in the scrubber and pass through flash separator using intermediate pressure to minimize the amount of natural gas entrained with the circulating glycol. Pre-heated glycol-water-natural gas is conducted to ease the separation process. The stipper is then introduced to increase the mola purity of glycol up to 99.9% for regeneration. As mention earlier, first fraction of gas will be used for lifting and seond fraction is liquefied to producing high quality NGL for sales.

3. Water Treatment

The treatment and cleaning of produced water are essential for legislation and environmental reason specifically in the North Sea region. The water treatment is carried out as the welltest performed in well 2 shows the high level of total dissolved solid (TDS) of 72 mg/l as required to be treated before disposal. The use of Hydrocyclones is commonly used, simpler and economical for the removal of suspended particulates and dissolved hydrocarbons. The wastewater is sent to skimmer tank through throttle succession and sent to hydrocyclone process before it released to the environment. As mentioned earlier, the treated water is used for water injection as pressure maintenance. However, the treated water must meet the quality requirements to prevent from pipe corrosion during the injection and bacteria formation because of the water containing the oxygen, sediments, and sulphides compounds.
